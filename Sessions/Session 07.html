Session 07 — Silk Du Killed / Heywood Sewers

← Session 06 · Session Log · Home · Session 08 →

Locations: Jianmen Subway Station, Heywood sewers (under San Morro Bridge)
Key Contacts: Sum Ting, One Shot Rodney
Gigs: Assassinate Silk Du (LCRA — 2000 eb each ✅); WFT cyber-shipment heist

Part 1 — Jianmen Subway Station: Silk Du

Resuming from where Session 06 left off — the crew had identified the WFT escalator access point.

They entered the Weng Fang Tong base via a flooded elevator shaft. Vyper forced open the elevator door.

Inside: Eastern Tiger Triad goons were out front — it turns out Silk Du had been trying to sell out his boss to the Eastern Tiger Triad.

A fight broke out in the lounge. Vyper stabbed Silk Du with the ceremonial Gold Dragon dagger. Pulse took a photo. The Maelstrom chased down the Weng Fang Tong remnants.

Mission complete. 2000 eb each.

Antoine upgraded Medtech level. Pinky upgraded Fixer to 1000 eb access.

Part 2 — Pressure Points / Heywood Sewers

A later session in this same arc — Asphalt Riot and the WFT cyber-shipment.

Asphalt Riot

Taking place under the brutal concrete belly of the Heywood Industrial Zone overpass — a sprawling, echoing cathedral of urban decay. Thashers, skaters, and couriers assembled for an underground event, lifting a collective finger to the corps.

Vyper performed — a Noise-Op. The rest of the crew ran side hustles.

The WFT Cybernetics Ambush

Intel (from the late Silk Du) placed a Weng Fang Tong cybernetics shipment coming through the Level 3 sewage conduits under the Morro from the Heywood Docks. These old tunnels once carried waste from the island; since the 4th Corporate War made the island's centre radioactive slag, they were wound back to partial capacity.

"...Not by air, not by road. They're coming through the abandoned Level 3 sewage conduits under the Morro from the Heywood Docks. My boss thinks he's being clever."

The crew accessed the tunnels via utility access points on the legs of the San Morro Bridge at water level. One Shot Rodney's contact, Three-Toed Joe (Thelas Nation sea-faring Nomads), ferried the group across the Morro for 500 eb.

What Went Down
