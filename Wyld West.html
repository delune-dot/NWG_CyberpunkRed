Wyld West

Home · Gig Board · Session Log

What Is It?

Wyld West is a cult television series — a supernatural Western set in a hellish, post-apocalyptic frontier. The party is currently in possession of Season 5, Episode 9: "What's in a Name" — the penultimate episode of the season.

This episode was obtained from the late Goto Dengo's Segotari account after raiding his Elflines lodge and completing the Bitteroot Frost Caves quest to claim the Sasquatch Fang (Sessions 10–11).

Season 5, Episode 9: "What's in a Name"

The penultimate episode of Season 5.

Setting: Hell — a vast, layered supernatural wasteland structured around railroad corridors and faction warfare.

The Premise: The Drifter, having refused Rail Baron Rockefeller's enticements, throws in with the mutant freedom fighters known as the Railroad. His plan: steal a Rockefeller locomotive, modify it with tech gathered across Hell, and drive it East — because to go West is to descend deeper into Hell, toward the Terminus.

The Drifter's Problem: He's been haunted by an inner split. In the Brimstone incident, two souls were fused into one body when the Darkstone blast dragged them to Hell together. One soul is a protector; the other is a dead-hearted killer. Only whiskey dulls the conflict within.

The Episode's Arc:

The expedition to recruit the mysterious 12th Cavalry (the Drifter's former military unit, now lost in the Blasted Wastes — the 4th layer of Hell) reveals the truth of this split.

One contingent of the 12th greets the Drifter as a hero, sharing campfire stories of the legendary Dave Davies — always there to pull them through, a guardian angel.

The second contingent is found in the Four Aces Saloon — suspicious, hands on irons. To them, Steve Stevens is a blight on the 12th: a cold-eyed killer who only cared for his weapons.

The duality revelation: Dave Davies and Steve Stevens are both the Drifter. Two identities. One body.

The 12th Cavalry's Guilt: The troopers, drawn in by the promise of freedom and absolution, confess their last standing orders before the Brimstone incident — they were commanded to corral hundreds of natives, drifters and pilgrims like cattle in makeshift pens on the outskirts of Brimstone. Before whatever happened, happened.

Supporting Cast:
- Yolo — Aztec guide providing an assembly location for the locomotive modifications
- The Railroad — secretive faction of "new abolitionists" handling logistics and supplies
- Rail Baron Rockefeller — antagonist; controls the locomotive infrastructure of Hell
- The 9th Cavalry — rejected as potential security due to ties with Rockefeller
- The 12th Cavalry — the Drifter's lost unit; their camp is the climax of the episode

Background: The Brimstone Incident

The show's core mythology. A Darkstone blast in the town of Brimstone dragged anyone caught in the blast radius into Hell. The resulting chaos merged some souls into composite identities, fusing their personalities and memories — but not peacefully.

The Drifter is one such composite, though neither personality knew the other existed. Season 5 is about confronting this fracture.
